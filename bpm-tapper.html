How to get an accurate reading

Tap on the strong beat, the one you would clap to, and keep a steady hand. The tool averages the gaps between your taps, so the more you tap the more stable the number becomes. After about six taps it is usually within two or three BPM of the real tempo.

Very fast songs

If a track feels too quick to tap every beat, tap on every other beat instead and double the result. The same trick works in reverse for slow ballads, where tapping each half beat and halving the number gives a steadier reading.

What the tempo words mean

The classical tempo markings are a quick way to describe feel. Largo is broad and slow, Andante is a walking pace, Moderato is middle of the road, Allegro is bright and fast, and Presto is very fast. They overlap and are interpreted loosely, but they give a useful shorthand for the number you just measured.

DJs and producers usually care about the exact figure for beatmatching and setting a session tempo. Once you have it, you can line up two tracks or program a drum pattern with confidence.
