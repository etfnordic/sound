What each waveform sounds like

A sine wave is the purest tone, a single frequency with no harmonics, ideal for testing and tuning. A square wave is hollow and buzzy because it is rich in odd harmonics. A triangle wave sits between the two, softer than a square but with more character than a sine. A sawtooth is the brightest, packed with every harmonic, which is why it cuts through so well in synth leads.

Testing speakers and headphones

Sweep slowly from low to high and listen for buzzes, rattles or sudden drops in level. A healthy system reproduces a sine smoothly across its range. If the bass disappears below a certain point, that is roughly your speaker's low-frequency limit.

Checking your hearing range

Most young adults hear up to around 18 to 20 kHz, and the upper limit drops gradually with age. Set a comfortable volume at 1 kHz, then raise the frequency and note where the tone fades to silence. Do the same at the low end, where many people stop perceiving pitch below about 20 Hz and start to feel it instead.

This is a fun reference, not a medical test. If you are worried about your hearing, see an audiologist. You can also measure how loud your environment is with the decibel meter.
